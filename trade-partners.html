Who we work with

Built for shops that need reliable metalwork

About half our throughput is trade subcontract. We work with shops across the northeast who need panel and structural work done properly, on time, and without the hassle of investing in a jig or dedicating bench space. Here is who typically sends work our way.

Paint and spray shops

You do the colour work. You know paint. But when a panel needs pulling, a quarter needs cutting or a sill needs welding before you can spray, that is where we come in. We prep the metal so you can lay down paint on a surface that is actually straight.

General garages

You service and repair cars all day. Occasionally one comes in with crash damage that is beyond what you can do in-house. Rather than turn the customer away, send the body work to us. You keep the customer, we do the metalwork, you handle everything else.

Mechanical specialists

You know engines, gearboxes, suspension. You do not want to invest in a chassis jig or hire a panel beater. We give you the ability to offer body repair to your customers without the capital outlay. Subcontract the metalwork, keep the margin on the full job.

Used car dealers

You have trade-ins that need tidying before they go on the forecourt. Dented wings, bent bumper bars, minor chassis pulls. We get them straight at trade rates so you can present them clean and move them on faster.

The process

How the trade process works

We keep it simple. No admin headaches, no unnecessary back-and-forth. Four steps from enquiry to completed job.

01

Call or send a photo

WhatsApp us photos of the damage or call to describe the job. We quote at trade rates the same day. No waiting around for an assessor to come out.

02

Drop off or we collect

Your customer drops the car at our workshop, you drop it yourself, or we collect within our 50km radius. Whatever works best for your operation.

03

We complete the metalwork

Panels straightened, chassis pulled, structural welds done. We supply documented before-and-after jig measurements on paper. Your customer gets proof the geometry is right.

04

You finish the job

You paint it, valet it, invoice your customer for the full job. We stay in the background. Your customer, your relationship, your margin.

We are not trying to compete with you. We are trying to make you money. Our trade rates are structured so you can subcontract the metalwork, add your margin, and still give your customer a fair price.

14 days net for established trade accounts. First job is payment on collection. Once we have a working relationship, we extend terms because that is how trade should work.

Documentation

Jig measurements supplied on paper

Every chassis and structural job comes with documented before-and-after measurements taken from the jig. Printed on paper, handed over with the car. This is not something most panel shops in the area do, and it matters.

Your customer gets documented proof that the chassis geometry has been brought back within manufacturer tolerance. That is something they can show to a future buyer, an engineer, or anyone who asks whether the car was repaired properly. It protects your customer, it protects you, and it protects us.

If you are a spray shop or garage subcontracting metalwork, this documentation sets you apart from competitors who cannot provide it. You are not just offering a repair — you are offering a verified repair with evidence to back it up.

Your customers stay yours

Confidentiality is not negotiable

This is simple and it is important, so we will say it plainly.

Your customers are your customers. We do not poach them. We do not market to them. We do not contact them after the job. We do not put our branding on anything you hand to the customer. As far as your customer is concerned, the work was done by your shop.

We have been on the other side of this. We know how it feels when a subcontractor tries to go around you. We do not do it. We will never do it. If we did, we would lose every trade partner we have, and rightly so.

You build the relationship with the customer. You take the credit. We do the metalwork in the background and send you the invoice. That is the deal, and we stick to it.

Trade questions

Frequently asked questions

What's your trade rate?

Every job is different, so we quote per job based on photos or inspection. For regular partners doing consistent volume, we offer day-rate arrangements that work out cheaper. Call or WhatsApp us with what you need and we will quote same day. No messing around.

Can you invoice my customer directly?

Yes, if that is how you want to work it. We can invoice you directly, invoice your customer on your behalf, or split the invoice however suits your setup. We are flexible — whatever makes your admin simpler.

Do you do fleet work?

Yes. If you manage a fleet or service fleet vehicles and need panel or structural repairs done on a regular basis, we can set up a standing arrangement with agreed rates and priority turnaround. We handle everything from light commercial vans to saloons.

Can I visit your workshop to see the operation?

Absolutely. We would encourage it. Come and see the jig, the setup and how we work. Most of our trade partners visited before they started sending work. Call ahead and we will have the kettle on.

Ready to send us your first job?

WhatsApp us photos of the damage and we will quote at trade rates the same day. Or call to talk through what you need. No obligation, no pressure — just a straight conversation between tradespeople.
