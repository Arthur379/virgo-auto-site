Why Carlingford drivers come to Virgo

Carlingford is one of the most beautiful spots in Co. Louth, but its charm comes with challenges for car bodywork. The village itself is built around narrow lanes that were laid out in the medieval period, and the surrounding Cooley Peninsula is served by the R173 — a winding coastal road that threads between the Cooley Mountains and Carlingford Lough. Stone walls, tight bends and oncoming traffic on roads barely wide enough for two vehicles create frequent scraping and low-speed impact damage.

During summer months, Carlingford draws large numbers of visitors for walking, sailing and the Greenway. The influx of unfamiliar drivers on narrow mountain roads adds to the number of bodywork incidents — hedge scrapes, wall clips and mirror strikes against passing vehicles. Locals who live on the peninsula year-round know these roads well, but even experienced drivers catch a gatepost or a stone wall now and then.

Carlingford has no panel beating workshop of its own. The nearest is in Dundalk, which is our location — about 25 minutes west along the R173 through Omeath and Ravensdale. We know the peninsula roads and regularly collect cars from the village, from the harbour area and from homes up along the mountain roads above the lough. Collection is free and we can bring a flatbed if the damage makes the car undrivable.

Services available to Carlingford customers

Panel beating

Wall scrapes, hedge damage and dents from the narrow Cooley roads — reshaped without over-reliance on filler.

Chassis alignment

Jig measurement and correction after kerb impacts, pothole damage or low-speed collision on mountain roads.

Structural repair

Sills, subframes and inner panels restored to factory standard after impacts that go beyond surface damage.

Frame straightening

Bench-mounted chassis correction for serious collision damage. Full documentation of before-and-after measurements.

Bumper & trim

Stone wall scrapes often catch bumper corners and wheel arch trim. We reshape and refit, ready for painting.

Trade subcontract

Metalwork for garages and mobile mechanics serving the Cooley Peninsula. Trade rates and documented work.

Getting to us from Carlingford

From Carlingford village, take the R173 west through Omeath. The road follows the southern shore of Carlingford Lough before climbing through Ravensdale and descending into Dundalk from the northeast. The drive is approximately 25 kilometres and takes about 25 minutes, though it can be slower in summer when tourist traffic picks up. An alternative route runs south from Carlingford through Greenore and along the coast road to Dundalk, though this adds a few kilometres. If your car cannot be driven, we will bring a flatbed — just ring ahead and we will arrange a time.

Frequently asked questions — Carlingford

The R173 from Carlingford is narrow — can you collect a damaged car from the village?

Yes. We regularly collect vehicles from the Carlingford and Cooley Peninsula area. We know the R173 well and can bring a trailer or flatbed if the car is not drivable. Collection from Carlingford is free — it is well within our 50km radius. Just let us know the location and we will sort the logistics.

I scraped a wall on one of the narrow streets in Carlingford — is that worth repairing?

Almost certainly. Wall scrapes on medieval village streets are one of our most common repair types from the Carlingford area. They usually damage the paint and leave shallow creases or gouges in the panel, but rarely bend the metal severely. A panel beater can reshape the metal and return it at pre-paint standard for far less than the cost of a replacement panel. Send us a photo and we will confirm.
