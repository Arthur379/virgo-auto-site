Why Monaghan drivers come to Virgo

Co. Monaghan is a rural border county with a landscape of drumlins, narrow roads and limited options for specialist body repair. The county town has garages that do mechanical work and general servicing, but finding a workshop with a dedicated panel bench and chassis jig is difficult without travelling. That is where we come in.

The N2 connects Monaghan town directly to Dundalk. The road has been upgraded significantly in recent years, and the 35-kilometre drive now takes about 30 minutes. For drivers in Castleblayney or Carrickmacross, the journey is even shorter — both towns sit between Monaghan and Dundalk on the N2 corridor, making them closer to our workshop than to most alternatives in Cavan or Armagh.

Monaghan's road network is predominantly single-carriageway with frequent agricultural traffic. Slow-moving machinery, loose gravel and blind bends create the kind of low-to-medium speed impacts that bend bodywork without destroying it — ideal candidates for panel beating rather than costly full-panel replacement. We also see cars from the border crossings around Emyvale and Aughnacloy where tight lanes and poor sightlines lead to wing and mirror damage.

Services available to Monaghan customers

Panel beating

Traditional metalwork to reshape dented panels. Hammer, dolly and shrinking disc — no filler shortcuts.

Chassis alignment

Bench-mounted jig correction with documented tolerances. Essential after any impact that shifts the chassis.

Structural repair

Welding, section replacement and reinforcement of structural components — sills, floors and inner wings.

Frame straightening

Full chassis pulls for serious front or rear collision damage. Rails, A-pillars and subframe mounts corrected.

Bumper & trim

Bumper reshaping, mounting bracket repair and plastic trim refit. Returned ready for primer and paint.

Trade subcontract

Metalwork for Monaghan-based garages and body repair operations. Trade pricing, jig documentation, discreet.

Getting to us from Monaghan

From Monaghan town, take the N2 southbound towards Castleblayney and Dundalk. The road is largely dual carriageway and the drive takes around 30 minutes. If you are starting from Castleblayney, the journey is shorter still — about 20 minutes. From Carrickmacross, head east on the R178 to join the N2 south of Castleblayney. We provide free collection for any vehicle within our 50-kilometre radius, so if your car has been left immobile after an incident on a Monaghan back road, ring us and we will sort the pickup.

Frequently asked questions — Monaghan

Are there specialist panel beaters in Monaghan town?

Monaghan has general garages and body shops, but dedicated panel beating and chassis alignment workshops are scarce in the county. Most bodyshops handle panel work as part of a broader service and may rely on filler rather than metalworking. If you want specialist panel beating with jig-based chassis correction, Virgo Auto in Dundalk is 35km away on the N2 — close enough for free collection.

Do you cover Castleblayney and Carrickmacross as well as Monaghan town?

Yes. Both Castleblayney and Carrickmacross are closer to our Dundalk workshop than Monaghan town itself. Castleblayney is about 25km via the N2, and Carrickmacross is around 28km via the N2 and R178. Free collection applies to all three towns and the surrounding rural areas.
